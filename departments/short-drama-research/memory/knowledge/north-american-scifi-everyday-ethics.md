For North American sci-fi short-drama concepts, the useful conversion pattern is not expensive spectacle but a familiar everyday setting suddenly governed by an abnormal technological or institutional rule: AI family replacement, survival credit scores, identity registration, automated denial, or invasive interviews.
The core engine should compress a large issue into a personal choice where both audience sides have plausible arguments, such as grief relief versus consent, resource efficiency versus dignity, or public safety versus identity persecution. A 20-episode arc can escalate from private abnormality to community conflict, institutional exposure, identity reversal, and a final irreversible moral choice. [[research-source-boundaries]]
