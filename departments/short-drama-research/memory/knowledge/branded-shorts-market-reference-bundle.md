The department owns a bilingual market reference at `artifacts/branded-shorts-market/`:
- `market-report-en.md` — English strategic summary covering format definition, market size (~$11B 2025 → ~$14B 2026 globally per Omdia; ~$1.4B U.S. 2025 per Owl & Co), production economics, distribution surfaces, brand-category map (Crocs, P&G Native, Marc Jacobs, Coverland, Samsung Brazil, Ibis, JCPenney/ViX, Maybelline, AJIO, Starbucks Douyin, Dhar Mann, etc.), what works vs failure modes, risks, and 10 strategic implications for our studio.
- `market-report-zh.md` — 中文版策略报告（同结构）。
**Consultation contract (must enforce when called):**
1. Pull, never push. Caller names the specific section ("market-report-en.md §3.5 Coverland") and records the consultation in their own work log.
2. Mechanism only — no script, dialogue, character constellation, title, or shot sequence is copied from sampled brand series. Generic genre vocabulary ("CEO-revenge microdrama") is OK; specific lines, casts, and beats are not.
3. Tag every imported claim as `[Observed]`, `[Candidate signal]`, or `[Inference]`. See [[claim-tagging-observed-candidate-inference]]. View counts, rankings, and lift figures default to `[Candidate signal]` until day-of-use re-verification.
4. Re-verify any "viral" claim on the day of use before external-facing artifacts. SAG-AFTRA Verticals Agreement sunsets June 30, 2026 unless extended — re-check date before any production-budget guidance.
5. Trope-toxicity re-frame still applies — possessive bond, captive-protector, humiliation arcs must preserve consent and agency or be rejected, even when the brand asks for them.
6. No platform circumvention — public watch and manual record only.
The bundle deliberately sits inside the department, not the workspace `skills/` folder, so it is not auto-suggested or auto-injected.
