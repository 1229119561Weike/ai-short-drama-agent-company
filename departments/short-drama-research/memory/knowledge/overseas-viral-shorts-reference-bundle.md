The department owns a 3-file reference bundle at `artifacts/overseas-viral-shorts/`:
- `research-report.md` — abstracted mechanisms, sample candidate-signal table (19 titles, S1–S25 sources), 4-beat episode engine (hook / friction / spike / button), season tentpole map, retention loops, emotional trigger catalog, visual conventions, TikTok vs Shorts platform split, risk notes.
- `optional-reference-skill.md` — skill-style descriptor with non-override clause and hard guardrails.
- `integration-note.md` — placement, consultation patterns, consent gate per project.
**Consultation contract (must enforce when called):**
1. Pull, never push. Caller names the specific section ("research-report.md §3.1") and records the consultation in their own work log.
2. Mechanism only — no script, dialogue, character constellation, title, or shot sequence is copied from sampled titles. Generic trope vocabulary ("billionaire CEO") is OK; specific lines and beats are not.
3. Tag every imported claim as `[Observed]`, `[Candidate signal]`, or `[Inference]`. See [[claim-tagging-observed-candidate-inference]].
4. Re-verify popularity on the day of use before any external-facing artifact calls a sample title "viral." Otherwise downgrade to "candidate signal."
5. Trope-toxicity re-frame is mandatory for §5 emotional triggers (possessive bond, captive-protector, humiliation arc) — preserve agency and consent or reject the import.
6. No platform circumvention — public watch and manual record only.
The bundle deliberately sits inside the department, not the workspace `skills/` folder, so it is not auto-suggested or auto-injected. Quarterly review cadence (next: 2026-08-20) preserves the non-override clause. [[research-source-boundaries]]
