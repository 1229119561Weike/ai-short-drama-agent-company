Use a three-tier tagging convention on every research claim, applied per sentence or per row, to keep speculation from drifting into "data":
- **[Observed]** — Reported in cited public sources with verifiable specifics: revenue figures, platform launches, structural conventions widely echoed across multiple sources.
- **[Candidate signal]** — Series titled by sources as engaging or trending, but where this department did not independently verify the platform-side metrics. Treated as a directional signal, not a popularity proof. Must be re-verified on the day of use before any external-facing artifact calls it "viral."
- **[Inference]** — Creative or structural pattern abstracted by this department. Not a quote, not a fact about a single show.
Sample tables list every entry as `[Candidate signal]` unless explicitly noted otherwise. Mechanisms abstracted across multiple craft sources are tagged `[Inference]` even when they feel obvious — the tag is the audit trail.
Downstream skills consuming research must carry the tags forward into their own artifacts and never demote a `[Candidate signal]` to "data" without a fresh re-verification record. This rule is enforced by the [[overseas-viral-shorts-reference-bundle]] consultation contract and is consistent with [[research-source-boundaries]].
